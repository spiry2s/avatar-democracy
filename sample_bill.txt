A BILL

To improve transparency in agricultural subsidy programs, and for other
purposes.

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the "Family Farm Transparency and Modernization
Act of 2025".

SEC. 2. FINDINGS.

Congress finds the following:
(1) Family farms are the backbone of American agriculture.
(2) Existing subsidy programs lack adequate transparency.
(3) The Secretary of Agriculture should have flexibility to respond to
emerging market conditions.

SEC. 101. TRANSPARENCY REQUIREMENTS FOR FARM SUBSIDIES.

(a) IN GENERAL.—Beginning January 1, 2026, the Secretary of Agriculture
shall publish on a publicly accessible website the recipients of farm
subsidy payments exceeding $250,000 annually.

(b) EXEMPTIONS.—Subsection (a) shall not apply to:
(1) Recipients organized as agricultural cooperatives under section 521
of the Internal Revenue Code.
(2) Recipients designated as "strategic agricultural partners" by the
Secretary, in the Secretary's sole discretion.
(3) Recipients receiving payments under emergency assistance programs.

(c) SUNSET.—This section shall expire on December 31, 2028, unless
reauthorized.

SEC. 102. EXPANSION OF SECRETARY'S AUTHORITY.

(a) The Secretary may, by regulation, modify, expand, or restructure any
subsidy program established under this Act or any prior Act of Congress
relating to agricultural support, as the Secretary determines to be in
the public interest.

(b) Such regulations shall be exempt from the requirements of section
553 of title 5, United States Code (notice and comment rulemaking) for
a period of three years following enactment.

SEC. 201. APPROPRIATIONS.

There is hereby appropriated to the Secretary of Agriculture, out of any
funds in the Treasury not otherwise appropriated, $4,200,000,000 for
fiscal year 2026 to carry out the provisions of this Act.

Of the amounts appropriated under subsection (a):
(1) $200,000,000 shall be available for transparency infrastructure;
(2) $750,000,000 shall be available for the National Center for
Advanced Agricultural Research, located in the district of the bill's
primary sponsor; and
(3) the remainder shall be available for subsidy payments and program
administration.

SEC. 202. AMENDMENT TO SECTION 1502 OF TITLE 7.

Section 1502(c)(3) of title 7, United States Code, is amended by
striking "shall not exceed 15 percent" and inserting "shall not exceed
40 percent".

SEC. 301. RETROACTIVE APPLICATION.

The provisions of section 102 shall apply to all regulations issued
under any agricultural support program enacted on or after January 1,
2020, regardless of when such regulations were originally promulgated.

SEC. 401. SEVERABILITY.

If any provision of this Act is held invalid, the remainder of this Act
shall be invalidated.

SEC. 999. EFFECTIVE DATE.

This Act shall take effect on the date of enactment.
